Overview

Neptune is the eighth and farthest planet from the Sun, the other ice giant. It has the strongest winds in the Solar System reaching 2,100 km/h. Discovered mathematically before telescopic confirmation based on perturbations to Uranus's orbit. Triton, its largest moon, is likely a captured Kuiper Belt object.

Active missions

Voyager 2 (NASA)
Flyby 1989-08-25
Only spacecraft to visit Neptune. Discovered 6 moons, the Great Dark Spot, and detailed the ring system. Mission success given 12-year travel time.
Trident (NASA, concept)
Proposed late 2020s
Discovery-class mission concept to study Triton.

Notable features

Great Dark Spot
Anticyclonic storm observed by Voyager 2 in 1989. Dissipated by 1994. New dark spots have appeared and disappeared since.
Triton
Largest moon. Retrograde orbit suggests it was captured from the Kuiper Belt. Has geysers of nitrogen gas, indicating subsurface activity.
Ring system
Five main rings named after Neptune discoverers. Contains ring arcs - denser segments held in place by Galatea.
Extreme winds
Unexplained given Neptune's distance from the Sun. Despite receiving only 0.1% of Earth's solar energy, wind speeds exceed all other planets.
